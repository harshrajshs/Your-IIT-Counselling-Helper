Welcome to our guide on choosing between New IITs and Old IITs. With over 20 IITs to choose from, it can be difficult to know which one to go for. We've collected data and analyzed it for you to make the decision-making process easier.

What's so special about Old IITs?

⬥Establishment
The first five IITs were established in the 1950s and 1960s, which makes them some of the oldest engineering colleges in India.
⬥Campus Size
The old IITs typically have larger campus sizes than the new IITs, with more infrastructure and facilities available.
⬥Faculty Experience
Old IITs boast of highly experienced and senior faculty members with decades of teaching experience.

What are New IITs?

⬥Establishment
New IITs were established more recently, in the past 10-15 years, as part of a government initiative to expand the IIT network.
⬥Campus Size
New IITs have smaller campus sizes compared to the older ones but are still equipped with modern infrastructure and facilities.
⬥Location
New IITs are located in newer cities with a better standard of living and accessibility, giving students ample opportunities.

Comparison between Old and New IITs based on Analytics

⬥Academics:
Old IITs have a richer history of producing top-notch researchers and have a higher volume of research publications per year.
⬥Faculty:
New IITs have more diverse faculty with a larger proportion of professors from international universities bringing in different views and methodologies.
⬥Quality of Campus Life:
Old IITs provide a more established student culture and have a well-settled alumni network. New IITs provide more diversity to the student community and have innovative cultures.

Conclusion and Final Thoughts

Choosing between an Old IIT or a New IIT depends on a variety of factors.

Based on our analysis, if you're more focused on research and academics, an old IIT might be a better fit. However, if you're looking for more diverse cultural experiences and unique opportunities, new IITs offer the same IIT brand experience on a modern infrastructure.
